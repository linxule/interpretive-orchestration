## Building Your Structure (Stage by Stage)

### Stage 1: Human-Led Foundation

**Start with 1st-Order Concepts (Bottom-Up)**

As you manually code 10-15 documents:

1. **Create codes that use informant language:**
   - ‚úì "I had to do something" (participant phrase)
   - ‚úì "Tracking what works" (how they describe it)
   - ‚úó "Moral Conviction" (too abstract for 1st-order)
   - ‚úó "Adaptive Strategies" (your interpretation, not their term)

2. **Keep codes close to the data:**
   - Quote: "I switched to just noting the really bad days"
   - Good 1st-order code: "Selective symptom tracking"
   - Too abstract: "Adaptive coping mechanisms"

3. **Accumulate until patterns emerge:**
   - You'll have 30-50+ 1st-order concepts initially
   - That's okay! Consolidation comes later

**Then group into 2nd-Order Themes:**

4. **Look for patterns across 1st-order concepts:**
   - Which concepts seem related?
   - What connects them theoretically?
   - What's the organizing logic?

5. **Name the theme with YOUR theoretical interpretation:**
   - This is your analytical abstraction
   - Should capture what unifies the 1st-order concepts
   - Example: 1st-order codes about trying, adjusting, personalizing ‚Üí 2nd-order theme "Adaptive Routine Building"

6. **Build initial aggregate dimensions (tentative):**
   - What overarching constructs organize your themes?
   - This is preliminary - will refine in Stage 2

**Stage 1 Output:**
- ~30-50 1st-order concepts
- ~8-12 2nd-order themes
- ~2-3 aggregate dimensions (initial)

---

### Stage 2: Refinement Through Collaboration

**Phase 1: Validate with broader patterns**
- Theoretical stream: Do dimensions align with literature?
- Empirical stream: Do concepts appear in extreme cases?

**Phase 2: Systematic coding reveals gaps**
- @dialogical-coder suggests refinements
- Some concepts get subdivided
- Some themes get consolidated
- New patterns emerge

**Phase 3: Pattern variations (if applicable)**
- Do systematic differences exist?
- Might need variant structures for different patterns
- Or: Properties/dimensions to capture variation

**Stage 2 Output:**
- Refined concepts (~30-80 depending on complexity)
- Clear 2nd-order themes (~10-15)
- Well-defined aggregate dimensions (~3-5)
- Each concept has multiple supporting quotes

---

### Stage 3: Theoretical Integration

**Final framework:**
- Parsimonious (fewer dimensions/themes than Stage 2)
- Theoretically novel (emphasizes contribution)
- Empirically grounded (every claim traceable to quotes)
- Literature-integrated (connected to existing theory)

## Common Questions

### Q: How many levels should I have?

**Three levels is standard Gioia method:**
- 1st-order = empirical
- 2nd-order = interpretive
- Aggregate = theoretical

**Some researchers add sub-levels:**
- Sub-themes under 2nd-order
- Properties/dimensions under concepts
- Fine if philosophically coherent!

### Q: How many dimensions/themes/concepts?

**No fixed rule, but typical:**
- Aggregate Dimensions: 3-5 (more becomes unwieldy)
- 2nd-Order Themes: 10-15 total across dimensions
- 1st-Order Concepts: 30-80 depending on phenomenon complexity

**Quality over quantity:** Better to have fewer well-developed concepts than many superficial ones.

### Q: Can concepts appear under multiple themes?

**Philosophically loaded question!**

**If your ontology:** Each concept has singular theoretical home
- Then: No, assign to most appropriate theme

**If your ontology:** Concepts can have multiple meanings
- Then: Yes, but document the multiple interpretations

**Check with /qual-examine-assumptions** if unsure!

### Q: When do I know my structure is complete?

**Stage 1:** When patterns stabilize (theoretical saturation)
**Stage 2:** When all documents coded and refinements incorporated
**Stage 3:** When theoretically parsimonious and publication-ready

**Not complete when:** New data would significantly change structure

## Philosophical Note

**This structure embodies Gioia & Corley epistemology:**

**NOT:** Objective categories discovered in data (Glaserian)
**BUT:** Systematic interpretive constructions grounded in evidence (Gioia)

**NOT:** Maximum reflexivity and multiple realities (Pure Charmaz)
**BUT:** Rigorous progression with interpretive honesty (Systematic interpretivist)

**Balance:** Empirical grounding + theoretical abstraction
**Goal:** Defensible contribution that readers can evaluate
