### When This Rule Is ACTIVE

You are using **parallel streams methodology** (common in Gioia approach). Theory and data should develop separately before synthesis:

1. **Stream A (Theoretical):** Develop your theoretical framework independently
2. **Stream B (Empirical):** Code your data inductively without forcing theory
3. **Keep streams separate** until the designated synthesis point
4. **Let each stream reach maturity** before integration

### Why This Matters

Premature theory-data integration undermines both:
- **Theoretical stream:** Becomes cherry-picked to match data
- **Empirical stream:** Becomes theory-confirming rather than theory-building

The power of the Gioia method comes from the **productive tension** between streams - each challenging and enriching the other at synthesis.

**Epistemological threat avoided:** Etic imposition, confirmation bias
**Validity concern addressed:** Theoretical contribution, analytical rigor

### What To Do Instead

**In Theoretical Stream:**
- Read broadly in your theoretical traditions
- Develop conceptual frameworks
- Note how theorists have approached similar phenomena
- Build your "theoretical sensitivity" without forcing it onto data

**In Empirical Stream:**
- Code inductively from participant language
- Let first-order concepts emerge from data
- Resist the urge to use theoretical labels prematurely
- Build second-order themes from empirical patterns

**At Synthesis:**
- Bring streams together with both fully developed
- Look for resonance, tension, and surprise
- Let theoretical integration emerge from the dialogue
