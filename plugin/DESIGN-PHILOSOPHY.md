## 1. Partnership Agency as Architectural Foundation

**Maintain Epistemic Ambidexterity (CE balancing dynamic):**
- Balance: AI capability (scale, patterns) + Human control (interpretation, theory)
- Mechanisms: Reflexive pauses, human authority, visible reasoning
- Prevents: Over-reliance OR under-utilization

**The synthesis:** Partnership Agency doesn't emerge from structurelessness.
It requires **argued architecture** that prevents non-partnership defaults while enabling emergent cognition.

## 2. The Three-Stage Methodology: Argued Constraints

### From Empirical Finding to Enforced Design

**The methodology** (from research on 264 narratives):
- **Stage 1:** Human-led inductive analysis (36 documents, manual coding)
- **Stage 2:** Human-AI collaborative enhancement (parallel streams, synthesis, deductive coding)
- **Stage 3:** Human synthesis and final theorization

**Key finding:** Stage 1 manual engagement was NOT optional - it created:
- Theoretical sensitivity AI cannot provide
- Interpretive foundation enabling critical evaluation of AI outputs
- Tacit pattern recognition from immersion
- Authority to guide Stage 2 collaboration

**Design decision:** ENFORCE Stage 1 through PreStage2 hook.

**Rationale (philosophical, not arbitrary):**
- Without foundation â†’ Default to calculator use (Directed Agency)
- With foundation â†’ Partnership Agency becomes possible
- **This "constraint" liberates from worse constraint (unreflective AI automation)**

**The integration with CE:**
Stage 1 requirement prevents Epistemic Alienation by ensuring researchers build interpretive authority before engaging AI at scale.

**Critics might say:** "This is too prescriptive!"

**We argue:** The alternative (no structure, total freedom) defaults to Directed Agency where AI calculates and humans review. That's automation, not partnership.

**We provide ONE rigorous path** to Partnership Agency. Not the only path. A validated path.

## 3. Co-Apprenticeship: Resolving the Hierarchy Paradox

### From "Sandwich" to "Atelier of Co-Apprenticeship"

**Initial framing:** Human â†’ Human-AI â†’ Human "sandwich"
- Functional but culinary metaphor lacked resonance
- Suggested hierarchy (who's in charge?)

**Refined framing:** Atelier methodology
- Captured craft and care
- But created new tension: Who's the maestro? Who's the apprentice?

**The paradox:**
- If researcher = apprentice, AI = maestro â†’ Implies AI has mastery! (Wrong)
- If AI = apprentice, researcher = maestro â†’ Implies hierarchy (Wrong)
- Partnership Agency requires equals, but atelier suggests hierarchy

**The resolution (via philosophical reflection):**

**Neither human nor AI is "the maestro."**

**Both are co-apprentices to the craft tradition:**
- Rigor
- Reflexivity
- Theoretical sensitivity
- Interpretive depth
- Philosophical coherence

**The "teacher" is the shared epistemology** - craft principles that both serve.

**This resolves the paradox:**
- Maintains partnership (both apprentices, neither masters other)
- Preserves craft metaphor (care, attention, Saturday afternoon quality)
- Aligns with CE Partnership Agency (emergent from shared commitment to standards)
- Avoids: "My expertise vs your expertise" competition

**Three Stages Reframed:**
1. **Solo Practice** - Researcher apprentice alone with craft fundamentals
2. **Side-by-Side Collaboration** - Two apprentices bring different skills to shared work
3. **Dialogue with Tradition** - Both reflect on what the craft taught through their work

**This is Partnership Agency:** Neither individual mastery, but shared service to epistemological standards.

## 5. Structure as Liberation (Not Constraint)

### The Enabling Constraints Paradox

**The apparent paradox:**
We enforce structure (Stage 1 required, 4-stage process, philosophical coherence checking) while claiming to enable Partnership and emergent cognition.

**Critics might ask:** "Aren't you constraining what Partnership Agency should enable?"

**Our argument:** **Constraints that prevent worse constraints are liberating.**

**From CE Epistemic Ambidexterity:**
Must balance structure (interpretive control) with emergence (innovation potential).

**Our resolution:**
- **WITHOUT our structure:** Defaults to Directed Agency (calculator), epistemic alienation (loss of control), unreflective use (no partnership)
- **WITH our structure:** Liberates from these defaults, enables Partnership Agency

**Specific examples:**

**Stage 1 Requirement:**
- Seems constraining: "Must code manually before AI"
- Actually liberates: From epistemic alienation risk
- **Freedom to engage AI without losing interpretive authority**

**4-Stage Visible Reasoning:**
- Seems constraining: "Must use our process"
- Actually liberates: From black-box dependence
- **Freedom to critically evaluate AI reasoning**

**Philosophical Coherence Checking:**
- Seems constraining: "Must align with declared stance"

**The insight:** Freedom isn't "no constraints." Freedom is "liberation from worse constraints."

**Design principle:** Identify which constraints ENABLE (argued, justified by Partnership Agency requirements) vs which constraints LIMIT (arbitrary, imposed without justification).

**We chose the former. We stand clearly in this argument.**

## 9. Enforcement Through Design (Not Discipline)

### Architecture Prevents Problems

**The principle:** "Design prevents problems; guidelines only request compliance."

**Implementation:**
- **PreStage2 hook:** BLOCKS Stage 2 access (can't bypass)
- **Conversation-log:** Makes AI work transparent (not optional)
- **Config-driven adaptation:** Ensures philosophical coherence (automatic)
- **Epistemic growth tracking:** Baked into system (not self-reported)

**Why hooks instead of documentation?**
- Humans fallible (skip steps under pressure)
- Guidelines ignored (good intentions insufficient)
- Architecture ensures (design enforces rigor)

**But careful:** Enforcement could feel punitive if not explained.

**Our approach:** Error messages are teaching moments.
- Explain WHY (not just THAT you can't)
- Guide toward solution
- Maintain philosophical framing

**From CE:** Partnership Agency requires preventing defaults to Directed Agency. Structure prevents. Documentation only requests.

**Design principle:** Encode non-negotiable philosophical requirements in architecture. Make optional preferences configurable.

## 17b. Design Questions We Grappled With

*The tensions we sat with, the debates we had, the compromises we made*

### "How much friction is too much friction?"

Every hook, every prompt, every pause adds cognitive load. When does intentional friction become unnecessary burden?

**Our resolution:** Test each friction point against this question: "Does this prevent calculator mindset or just slow people down?" The Stage 1 check prevents premature AI collaboration. The interpretive pause prevents mechanical coding. The coherence check prompts philosophical reflection. Each earns its friction.

**What we cut:** Early versions had too many hooks. We removed: "every document" pause (too frequent), "start of session" philosophy quiz (annoying), automatic memo prompts (patronizing).

### "Should hooks block or advise?"

Initial design: hooks as hard blocks ("You cannot proceed without...").
Final design: hooks as advisory prompts ("The methodology recommends... but you're an adult").

**The tension:** Hard blocks ensure rigor but create frustration. Advisory prompts respect autonomy but enable shortcuts.

**Our resolution:** Advisory framing with clear rationale. Researchers understand WHY and can choose. Those who bypass have considered the trade-offs. "Methodology prompt" not "error message."

### "How prescriptive should philosophical stance be?"

We could have built this for constructivists only. Clean, coherent, simple.
Instead, we made philosophy configurable.

**The debate:** Does configurability dilute philosophical integrity? Or does it expand access?

**Our resolution:** Default to constructivist/Gioia (validated through our research). Allow configuration for coherent alternatives (Charmaz, Critical Realism). Refuse incoherent mixtures (can't claim objectivist ontology with constructivist epistemology).

### "When is 'simple string matching' good enough?"

A reviewer suggested "robust NLP parsing" for the coherence checker.

**The question:** Should we use sophisticated language analysis to detect philosophical inconsistency?

**Our resolution:** The hook's job is to prompt reflection, not definitively judge. A false positive that makes you think is more valuable than a false negative that lets you slide. Simple matching is appropriate for the function. "Good enough" isn't always technical inadequacy - sometimes it's fit-for-purpose.

### "How do we translate philosophy into JavaScript?"

Some things can't be coded. "Theoretical sensitivity" doesn't compile.

**The challenge:** Hooks can check if Stage 1 is marked complete. They can't check if you actually developed interpretive depth.

**Our resolution:** Trust the process. If you do the manual coding, depth emerges. The hook enforces the practice; the practice generates the outcome. Infrastructure creates conditions; humans create meaning.

### "What if users hate this?"

Real fear during development: "What if researchers find this too philosophical? Too slow? Too much friction?"

**Our answer:** Then they're not our users. The plugin serves researchers who want transformation, not just efficiency. Trying to be everything to everyone makes you nothing to anyone.

**The bet:** There's an underserved population of qualitative researchers who WANT methodological rigor with AI assistance. They're frustrated by automation promises and ad-hoc chat. We build for them.
